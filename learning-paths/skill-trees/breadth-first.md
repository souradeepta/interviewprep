# Breadth-First Skill Tree

**Level:** L3-L5
**Time to read:** ~10 min

**Philosophy:** Get exposure to all domains first, then specialize.

This path is ideal if you want **balanced coverage** and the ability to recognize problem types across all domains.

---

## Path: Sample All, Then Deepen

### Week 1: Introduction to All Domains (2-3 hours)
Pick ONE EASY problem from each of these 12 domains:
- Arrays, Strings, Linked Lists, Stacks/Queues
- Trees, Graphs, Heaps, Hash Tables
- Dynamic Programming, Sorting/Searching
- Bit Manipulation, Design Patterns

**Goal:** Recognize problem types, know which domain applies

### Week 2: Deepen on All Domains (5-6 hours)
Pick ONE MEDIUM problem from each domain:
- All 12 domains, one medium each

**Goal:** Understand core patterns, feel comfortable with each topic

### Weeks 3-4: Identify Weaknesses (2-4 hours)
Self-assessment:
- Which 3 domains felt hardest?
- Which 3 domains felt easiest?

Plan Weeks 5-8:
- **Weak domains:** 2 more problems each (3 domains)
- **Medium domains:** 1 more problem each (6 domains)
- **Strong domains:** Skip or do hard variants

### Weeks 5-8: Specialize

Choose your specialization path:

**Track A: Breadth across all** (balanced)
- Continue with 1 medium/hard from each domain
- Best for: well-rounded interview prep

**Track B: Specialize in 3** (focused)
- Weak domains: All remaining problems
- Other domains: Skip or do easy only
- Best for: role-specific preparation

**Track C: Pick your role** (specialized)
- **Frontend:** Strings, design patterns, UI state
- **Backend:** Trees, graphs, system design, databases
- **Data:** Dynamic programming, bit manipulation, sorting

---

## When to Use Breadth-First

✅ **Good for:**
- Quick interview prep (4-6 weeks)
- Well-rounded preparation
- Startup/early-stage interviews
- First-time interview prep

❌ **Not ideal for:**
- FAANG deep technical bar
- Very limited exposure (< 2 weeks)
- Specializing in one domain

**Day 6:** Graphs easy

---

## Sample Week 2-4 Schedule

**Week 2:** One medium from each domain (1 per day × 12)  
**Weeks 3-4:** Self-assess weaknesses and plan specialization

---

## Success Tips

1. **Don't get stuck** — If a problem takes > 30 min, skip and return later
2. **Track weaknesses** — Note which domains are hardest
3. **After week 2, customize** — Weeks 3-8 depend on YOUR needs
4. **Use interview playbooks** — Focus on what matters for your interview stage
5. **Broad then deep** — Maintain breadth while going deep in weak areas

---

## Weekly Pace Calculation

**Week 1:** 2-3 hours (1 easy from each domain)  
**Week 2:** 5-6 hours (1 medium from each domain)  
**Weeks 3-4:** 2-4 hours (self-assess)  
**Weeks 5-8:** 6-8 hours (specialize)  

**Total:** 20-27 hours (4-5 hours/week)
